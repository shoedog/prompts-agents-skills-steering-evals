# Repository Overview

This repository follows a layered architecture that has evolved organically over several years of active development. At its core, the service is a Python application built to handle inbound requests, transform and persist data, and expose results through a set of well-defined interfaces. The team has generally favored clarity and convention over cleverness, and much of the code reflects that sensibility, with descriptive names and a preference for explicit flow over implicit magic.

The application is organized into several conceptual layers. The presentation layer sits at the top and is responsible for accepting requests, performing lightweight validation, and shaping responses for consumers. Beneath it lives the service layer, where the bulk of the business logic resides. This is where the interesting decisions happen, and where most of the domain knowledge is encoded. Below that sits the data-access layer, which mediates between the service layer and the various persistence backends the system relies upon. Cross-cutting concerns such as logging, configuration, and authentication are woven throughout, and the team has historically debated whether these belong in dedicated modules or closer to the code that uses them.

Data generally flows in one direction. A request arrives at the edge, is authenticated and validated, and is then handed to a service that orchestrates the work. Services tend to be moderately sized and are composed of smaller collaborators, each with a fairly narrow responsibility. When persistence is required, the service delegates to a repository abstraction, which hides the details of the underlying store. Results propagate back up through the same layers, gathering any presentation-specific formatting along the way before finally being returned to the caller.

Configuration is handled through a combination of environment variables and configuration files, with sensible defaults baked in for local development. The team has tried, with varying degrees of success, to keep environment-specific concerns out of the core logic. Observability is provided through structured logging and a modest collection of metrics, and there is an ongoing conversation about how much tracing the system truly needs versus how much simply adds noise.

Historically, the codebase has grown in bursts, often in response to shifting product priorities. As a result, some corners are more polished than others, and a handful of modules carry the fingerprints of older conventions that the team has since moved away from. Newcomers usually find the overall shape approachable, though they occasionally stumble over the parts of the system that predate the current structure. On the whole, the repository aims to be a comfortable place to work: familiar in its patterns, unsurprising in its layout, and forgiving of the ordinary mistakes that come with everyday development.
